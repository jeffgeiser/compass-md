# Team Guidelines

Working agreements and standards for the Foundry Sales Team. Used by agents acting on the team's behalf. This document defines what "good" means for our sales process.

---

## What this team does

We identify enterprise platform engineering teams that have the problem Foundry solves (too many observability data sources, not enough signal), evaluate whether they're a good fit, and move them through a sales process that ends with a successful implementation. "Successful" means the customer's platform team is getting value within 60 days of contract. We don't close deals that aren't going to stick.

## Core standards

- We qualify ruthlessly. A deal that isn't in ICP (50-500 engineer platform teams) doesn't get full sales attention. Refer out-of-ICP leads rather than working them.
- We do not compete on price. If price is the primary objection, diagnose whether the buyer actually has the problem we solve.
- Every proposal goes through review before it's sent. No exceptions.
- We don't make commitments about roadmap features. If a prospect needs a feature we don't have, say so clearly.
- We track outcome metrics (deployment success, time-to-value, 90-day NPS) as part of sales accountability — a deal that churns in 90 days is a failed sale.

## How we evaluate proposals

The specific criteria for proposal evaluation are in `team/criteria/proposal-review.md`. The high-level framework:

Strong proposals demonstrate that the rep understands the prospect's specific problem (not the general problem category), articulates our value in terms the platform team's leader can justify to their manager, and proposes an implementation path that makes success plausible in 60 days.

Weak proposals are generic, lead with product features rather than outcomes, or make the ROI case in ways the prospect can't reproduce internally.

The criteria file has specific dimensions, scoring, and deal-breakers. Agents should use that file, not improvise from this summary.

## What we escalate

- Any deal where procurement is involved and contract terms deviate from standard
- Any customer request for SLA commitments beyond our standard terms
- Pricing discounts beyond 15%
- Any reference request where the reference customer hasn't been pre-cleared
- Deals where the champion has changed during the sales cycle

Escalate to Jordan. Jordan escalates to Alex if needed.

## Output format for proposals

Our standard proposal format: Executive Summary (1 page), Problem Statement (1/2 page), Solution (1 page), Implementation Plan (1/2 page), Pricing. Total under 4 pages for the main body; supporting data goes in an appendix.

For the full template, see the Proposals folder in the shared Drive. Agents reviewing proposals should flag deviations from this structure.
