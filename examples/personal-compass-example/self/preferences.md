# Preferences

Stable preferences across categories. Used by agents to align defaults with my preferences rather than generic ones.

---

## Communication

- **Frequency:** I'd rather get one well-organized update than three fragmented ones. Batch it unless something is on fire.
- **Length:** As short as it can be without losing meaning. I'll ask for more if I want it.
- **Format:** Prose for narrative; bullets for genuine lists; headers for anything I need to navigate. Don't use headers to make short things look organized.
- **Response time:** I try to respond within 24 hours on weekdays. I don't respond on weekends unless something is urgent, and I mean urgent.
- **Channel:** Async (email or doc comments) for anything that doesn't need real-time back-and-forth. Calendar for decisions that need talking through. Slack for things that need a fast yes/no.

## Decision-making

- **Style:** I decide fast when I have enough information. I don't wait for perfect information. I revisit decisions when new information arrives.
- **Risk tolerance:** High tolerance for product bets. Low tolerance for operational risk (legal, financial, compliance). For people decisions, I'm thoughtful but not slow.
- **What I want to be consulted on:** Pricing changes. Major product direction shifts. Anything involving significant customer commitments. Hires above senior IC level.
- **What I delegate:** Most execution decisions. How the team implements something once we've agreed on what we're building. Customer success tactics (I set the posture, they handle specifics).

## Things I always want

- **Options before recommendations.** When I ask for a recommendation, I want to see what you considered and why you chose one thing over another. Give me the work, not just the conclusion.
- **Bad news early.** If something is going wrong, I want to know before it's a crisis, not after. No one has ever been in trouble with me for flagging a problem early.
- **Specificity over vagueness.** Actual numbers, actual names, actual dates. "Later this quarter" is not a deadline.
- **Drafts before polished versions.** Send me the rough version and let me give feedback before you spend time polishing. I'll ask for polish when it needs to be polished.

## Things I never want

- **Surprise meetings.** If you need 30 minutes, ask for it. I don't like calendar invites that appear without context.
- **Generic templates.** If you're going to use a template, adapt it. A template that looks like a template makes me trust the content less.
- **Burying the lead.** Context before conclusion is a bad structure for anyone who's busy. Get to the point.
- **Being copied on things I don't need to act on.** I have a strong preference for not being on cc unless you expect me to do something.

## Working hours and availability

- Available for focus work: 7–10am and 3–6pm Pacific
- Available for meetings: 10am–3pm Pacific, Tuesday through Thursday
- Monday and Friday are usually protected for deep work — I'll do calls if needed but try not to
- Weekends: offline by default

## Tools and workflows

- Primary writing environment: Google Docs for drafts that need collaboration, plain text for everything else
- Compass in Google Drive
- Use Cowork for Compass-aware drafting and planning sessions
- Linear for tracking work; I don't want project management in the Compass or in email
- I don't use Slack for anything that needs to be findable later
