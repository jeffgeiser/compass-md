## 2026-03-15 — Hired Jordan as first dedicated sales hire

**Decision:** Brought on Jordan (ex-Datadog, 8 years enterprise sales) as our first full-time sales hire, starting Q2 2026. Equity-heavy comp structure.

**Context:** I've been doing all customer conversations myself since founding. We have 40+ inbound leads in the queue that I haven't been able to follow up on properly. Chose between: continuing founder-led sales longer, hiring a junior SDR, or hiring an experienced AE. Also considered a fractional head of sales.

**Reasoning:** The constraint isn't lead generation — it's capacity to run discovery and close cycles at the enterprise level. A junior SDR would have added top-of-funnel capacity but not closed. Fractional would have added strategic advice but not execution. Jordan brings both pattern recognition and the willingness to run the full motion. The equity-heavy structure means Jordan is incentivized to build the process, not just use whatever I built.

**Outcome:** TBD — starting Q2. Early signal from reference calls was strong.

## 2026-02-01 — Decided against freemium model

**Decision:** Will not pursue a freemium tier. Staying with the current sales-assisted trial model.

**Context:** Q4 board conversation raised the question of freemium for product-led growth. I spent January researching how comparable infrastructure tools have used freemium. Considered: (a) building a freemium tier ourselves, (b) partnering with a PLG agency to design and implement it, (c) staying the course with sales-assisted trials.

**Reasoning:** Our buyers are platform engineering teams at companies with 50-500 engineers. They don't self-serve for infrastructure tooling — procurement and security review happen regardless of whether we have a free tier. Freemium would create a maintenance burden (a separate product tier) without changing the buying motion. The tools that have succeeded with freemium in our space (e.g., early Datadog) had a fundamentally different motion — bottom-up developer adoption leading to top-down licensing deals. We don't have the developer community or the viral mechanics to make that work. Staying sales-assisted keeps us focused on enterprise ACV.

**Outcome:** Correct so far. New enterprise deals closing faster with the sales-assisted model than the comparative data for PLG tools in our segment.

## 2025-11-10 — Decided to delay Series A

**Decision:** Not raising Series A in Q1 2026 as originally planned. New target: Q3 2026 when we have 8-10 enterprise logos and a demonstrable sales playbook.

**Context:** We had 3 inbound conversations with seed-stage investors who were interested in leading a Series A. The market timing felt right but our metrics told a different story — strong retention, weak new logo growth. Raising on the current numbers would mean raising at a lower valuation than we think is achievable in 6 months.

**Reasoning:** We have 18 months of runway. Using it to build the enterprise story is worth more than the cost of waiting. The enterprise deals we're close on will create a better narrative for the A than anything we have today. The risk of waiting is that the market changes or we slip — we've modeled both scenarios and they're manageable.

**Outcome:** TBD. On track as of April 2026.

## 2025-09-01 — Narrowed ICP to platform teams at 50-500 person companies

**Decision:** Focused ICP specifically on platform engineering teams (not general DevOps, not DevSecOps) at companies with 50-500 engineers. Stopped pursuing enterprise deals outside this range.

**Context:** We had customers at 5-person startups, 40-person growth companies, and one 2,000-person enterprise. The 2,000-person enterprise was technically a customer but the deal was painful and the renewal was uncertain. The small companies churned when they couldn't hire someone to own the tool.

**Reasoning:** The 50-500 engineer range has a predictable buying pattern (platform team exists and has budget), a consistent problem (too many data sources, not enough signal), and predictable churn drivers (none, as long as the platform team is empowered). Outside this range, we were solving different problems for different buyers. Focusing ICP means we can build a sales playbook instead of reinventing each deal.

**Outcome:** Correct. Win rate in ICP segment is significantly higher than historical overall win rate. Churn in ICP segment is essentially zero.
