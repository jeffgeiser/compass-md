# Examples

Populated Compass examples. Use them to understand what a working Compass looks like — what level of detail is useful, how voice files differ from perspective files, how the team Compass structures shared knowledge.

These are constructed examples (not real people's data), but they're built to be realistic. The goal is that you could hand one to someone who'd never heard of compass-md and they'd understand the convention from reading it.

---

## Personal Compass: Alex

[`personal-compass-example/`](personal-compass-example/)

Alex is a B2B SaaS founder running a company called Foundry. The Compass covers Alex's voice (dry, direct, no corporate clichés), perspectives on competitive strategy, hiring, and pricing, and a few decisions including the anti-freemium choice.

Read this example to understand:
- What a populated `voice.md` actually looks like vs. a template
- How `decisions.md` captures reasoning, not just outcomes
- What a good perspective file looks like — specific enough to actually inform agent behavior
- How the `log.md` records Compass activity
- What pending refinements look like in practice

## Team Compass: Proposal Review

[`team-proposal-review-example/`](team-proposal-review-example/)

A sales team's shared Compass supporting the proposal-review workflow from the main README. The team Compass has no `self/` folder — it's pure team context used by agents reviewing proposals, logging feedback, and proposing guideline updates.

Read this example to understand:
- How `team/guidelines.md` differs from `team/criteria/proposal-review.md`
- What a populated `feedback_log.md` looks like
- How refinements in a team context propose guideline updates (not personal voice updates)
- What team `style.md` captures that individual voice files don't

---

## These are starting points, not prescriptions

Real Compasses look different from these examples. Alex has three perspective files; you might have one or twenty. The team Compass has a specific structure for proposal review; your team's recurring task has different dimensions.

The convention is the structure. The content is yours.
